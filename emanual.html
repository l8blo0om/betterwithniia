The spine series — articulation, mobilization and decompression of the whole spine, taught on the pole/strap learning versions and then on the handle unit (Final Format).

Arch and Curl

Arch and Curl (Pole)

Lineage Pole learning version — becomes the handle-unit Arch and Curl.

Setup Straddle the bench facing the tower, pole held in front. Feet on the floor or bench edge. Press the pole in toward you and slightly up — hold it active, never resting on the body or hanging on the cables. Alternate hold: pole under the arms, elbows bent, hands reaching forward.

The movement Curl first — drop the chin, round the spine forward from the top down, vertebra by vertebra, drawing the belly back as the pole travels away. Then arch — open the chest, lengthen the front of the body, lead from the breastbone into extension, not from the low back. Curl flows into arch, arch back into curl, no stop between.

Breath Exhale as you curl and hollow forward; inhale as you arch and open.

What you're teaching Wakes the spine segment by segment through its full forward-and-back range. Releases the shoulders and arms; gentle stimulation for heart and lungs. The motion should look like a wave, not a fold.

Benefits Articulates and mobilizes the whole spine evenly; decompresses between the vertebrae; frees the shoulders and starts the breath/circulation pump.

Common errors & corrections

  • Resting or hanging on the pole → keep it active, pressed in and up.

  • Arching from the low back → lead from the sternum.

  • Moving in big blocks → articulate segmentally.

  • Holding the breath through the transition → let breath drive the shape.

Modifications Pole under the arms for those who can't grip comfortably.

Sideways Arch (Pole)

Lineage Pole learning version — the lateral arch.

Setup Straddle the bench facing the tower, pole active. Palms can support the opposite side of the head, fingertips hooked into the dimple behind the ear.

The movement Lengthen up and arch laterally over to one side, the spine making a long even C, then return through upright and over to the other side. Balanced position: side of neck over the pubic bone.

Breath Inhale toward upright, exhale into the side arch.

What you're teaching Even lateral extension of the whole spine, not a crease at the waist.

Benefits Opens and lengthens the side body; lateral decompression and even segmental spacing.

Common errors & corrections

  • Folding at the waist → reach long and even.

  • Letting the pelvis tip → keep the base level.

Modifications Hands on thighs instead of behind the head.

Lineage Pole learning version — rotation.

Setup Straddle the bench facing the tower, pole active, sit tall and elevated.

The movement From a lifted spine, rotate around the central axis, turning segment by segment while keeping length, then unwind back to upright. Balanced spiral: whole spine aligned and upright.

Breath Inhale to upright, exhale into the rotation.

  • Leading with the head only → turn from the center.

Dolphin

Dolphin forward & reverse

Lineage Hamstring Final Format.

Setup Seated facing the tower, back supported, feet in the cables, hands resting at the grips, spine long.

The movement Reach the legs long and low away from you, then draw them back under so the leg line arcs down and through — like a dolphin diving. Forward reaches the arc away; reverse runs the same arc the other direction.

Breath Exhale as the legs reach long and away; inhale as they gather back under.

What you're teaching Length through the back of the legs without gripping the low back; the legs moving from a quiet, stable pelvis.

Benefits Lengthens and articulates the back of the legs and hamstrings along a continuous arcing path; builds supple, coordinated leg reach without gripping the low back.

Common errors & corrections

  • Low back arching as the legs reach → keep the belly drawing back, pelvis quiet.

  • Yanking from the cables → let the legs lead, hands just guiding.

Modifications Smaller arc; more back support.

Frog Preparation 1

Frog prep 1 (Hamstring, on the bench, upper straps on feet)

Lineage Hamstring Series; Progression 2 new exercise (p129). First of two preps building toward the Frog.

Setup Lying on the bench facing away from the tower, upper straps on the feet. Starting position: extended legs in parallel, together, pointed feet.

The movement Double knee bend (as in double leg pump), shinbone parallel to the cable line, pointed feet; open the legs into a diamond shape, finding the individual diamond-shape position - if possible shinbone parallel to the floor, initiating by narrowing of the pelvis with the sit bones reaching forward; then extend the legs following the cable line diagonally down, from turned-out into parallel on the way.

Breath Exhale into the opening and the extension.

What you’re teaching Finding the diamond shape and the narrowing-led opening of the legs before the full Frog.

Benefits Opens the hips into the diamond; trains the narrowing-led leg opening; prepares the Frog kick.

Common errors & corrections

  • Losing the narrowing as the legs open - initiate from the pelvis.

  • Knees collapsing rather than tracking into the diamond.

Modifications Hands-on: same as double leg pump; support the narrowing - Grip 1 traction and open the knees, Grip 2 guiding from the ankle (wrapping around the ankle).

Frog

Frog

Lineage Hamstring Series; confirmed Progression 2 (pp129-130), preceded by Frog preps 1 and 2.

Setup Lying on the bench facing away from the tower, upper straps on the feet. Parallel legs, pointed feet, turned out.

The movement From parallel pointed turned-out legs, move into the diamond shape; open the legs to the side with the knees staying in the same position as in the diamond shape, pointed feet; flexed feet in the maximum opened position; then turn the legs out and close together. Final Frog: double breath for the slower version, single breath for the continuous version.

Breath Double breath (slower version) or single breath (continuous version).

What you’re teaching Hip opening with hamstring and inner-thigh length, legs symmetrical, through the diamond-to-V path.

Benefits Opens the hips; lengthens hamstrings and inner thighs; symmetrical leg coordination.

Common errors & corrections

  • Knees collapsing in - keep them tracking open.

  • Low back arching on the press - keep the force out of the upper body.

Modifications Frog preps 1 and 2 first. Hands-on: palms wrapping around the ankle plus traction; guide legs (pulling action from the legs), check the position of shinbone, ankles, pelvis; traction on the knees with the knees staying in place; brush along the shinbones; arrange the feet, brush along the inside of the legs.

Reps / tempo Mini-series: connect Frog prep 1, Frog prep 2, and Frog.

Canoeing

Canoeing (Upper Body Series, upper straps; new exercise p136)

Lineage Upper Body Series; Progression 3 new exercise (p136), built with two preps. Five-position continuous movement.

Setup Sitting on the bench facing the tower, upper straps in the hands.

The movement Five linked positions: (1) Scooping into arch toward the tower, scooping the arms, arch the spine, look up; (2) spiraling twist in bowed curl position, body initiates and the arms follow with delay, ribcage connected, leading arm placed as in simple twist next to the bench top (no need to touch), second arm follows the guiding arm; (3) MODERATE side shift of the ribcage, leading arm scooping sideways, look up and over the leading arm, second arm follows, weight shift of the body to the scooping side (accommodating shift); (4) long bowed position, unwind into facing the tower, fingers pointing forward and down = “dracula position,” arms parallel with elbows bent, look slightly up toward the hands; (5) diving down, palms brushing in front of the knees, transition into scooping up into arch.

Breath Add the breathing last — teacher breathes with the student. Counting in 4s: and down – and shift – and center – and dive.

What you’re teaching The body guides and the arms follow with delay throughout — a continuous canoe-paddle of the whole torso through arch, twist, side shift, unwind, and dive.

Benefits Whole-torso diagonal coordination; spiraling and side-shift control with arm delay; links arch, curl, twist and dive into one flowing upper-body sequence.

Common errors & corrections

  • Arms leading instead of the body → body guides, arms follow with delay.

  • Losing the ribcage connection in the twist.

  • Head out of position in the side shift.

Modifications Five teaching steps: (1) learn each position of the spine with the leading arm; (2) learn the spiraling twist in the bowl curl position, one arm guiding and one following — attention to hand placement, body guiding, head position; (3) learn the side-shift position — attention to head position; (4) entire movement in slow motion in 4 counts; (5) add the breathing. Hands-on: standing next to the student support the leading arm; sitting behind, palms next to the bench, instruct the student to touch the palm for placement of the leading arm; then guide the ribcage / support the scoop / support the unwinding into center / support the diving.

Progressions — the ladder

The seven series are the vocabulary; the progressions sequence that vocabulary into a client journey. This studio’s manual builds the beginner through STEP 1 to STEP 6 “towards Progression 1” — completing Step 6 is the completion of Progression 1 — then opens Progression 2 and Progression 3, each in three steps. The ladder in this manual ends at Progression 3; the higher tiers below are flagged as not present here. The Creative Series sits beyond, for certified trainers.

Foundation — overview

What this develops The beginner course, built session by session as STEP 1 through STEP 6 “towards Progression 1.” Each step carries a theme and layers the base vocabulary across all seven series. Completing Step 6 IS the completion of Progression 1 — Foundation and Progression 1 are the same arc, not two separate tiers.

Adds at this level Step 1 (the base), Step 2 (Breath), Step 3 (Legs / engagement), Step 4 (Details / vocabulary), Step 5 (Narrowing of the pelvis), Step 6 (Break it down + put it together — completion of Progression 1).

The six steps are the Foundation course; finishing them completes Progression 1. Confirmed against the manual pages 98–125.

Foundation Step 1

What this develops The base. Establishes the beginner vocabulary across the series sitting and on the unit.

Adds at this level Spinal Motions (Arch–Curl, Spiral, Sideways Arch, Wave forward) sitting on a chair. Arch & Curl: Preparation with the handle unit, Articulating one arm, Stirring. Hamstrings: Preparation on the floor (alignment, 5th line, single leg pump, shivering, kneading in 3s), then Double leg pump, Single leg pump, Dolphin forward, Single leg bicycle forward. Foundation Step 2

Vocabulary

The working words of the method, in plain language, with the exercises that call on each principle.

Arch

also: extension, arching

Extending the spine backward — opening the chest and lengthening the front of the body, leading from the breastbone rather than cranking the low back. Balanced Arch checkpoint: back of head over the sacrum.

Curl

also: flexion, rounding, curling

Rounding the spine forward from the top down, vertebra by vertebra, drawing the belly back. Balanced Curl checkpoint: throat center over the pubic bone.

Used in Arch and Curl · Simple Curl Ups · Curl Ups with Leg Combination · Twist and Curl Over · Arch and Curl: extroverted · Arch and Curl: introverted · Figure Eights · Cross Grip Stretch · Lobster Tail · Curl Ups: Preparation on the floor · Cradle Combination · Standing on all fours · Curl Ups into Arch: bent legs · Curl Ups into Arch: extended legs · Simple Curl Ups crossing over · Curl Ups crossing over into Arch: bent legs · Curl Ups crossing over into Arch: extended legs · Jackknife with feet on the bench · Chest - Pelvis Lift: arms on the side · Psoas Abdominals: Knee to forehead Combination · Sequence 3: Squeeze in fetal position

Articulation

also: segmental, vertebra by vertebra

Moving the spine one vertebra at a time so the motion looks like a wave rather than a fold — even distribution through all the segments.

Used in Arch and Curl · Chest - Pelvis Lift: arms on the side · Sideways Arch · Twist and Curl Over · Wave · Arch and Curl: extroverted · Arch and Curl: introverted · Figure Eights · Double Spiral · Lobster Tail · Wave (Upper Body)

Wave

also: rolling

Continuous, fluid, full-spine articulation rolling through the segments rather than hinging at a few points.

Used in Wave · Wave (Upper Body) · Arch and Curl

Spiral / Spiraling

also: rotation, twist, twisting, rotate

Axial rotation around the central plumbline with length — turning segment by segment without collapsing. Balanced Spiral checkpoint: whole spine aligned and upright. Common attention: start and return to upright; inhale toward upright, exhale to the extremes; rhythm 'and 1, 2, 3'; crave space between the vertebrae; even distribution.

Used in Twist · Single Spiral · Double Spiral · Cross Grip Stretch into Spiral · Twist and Curl Over · Dolphin Sideways: forward · Simple Twist and Pull with leaning back · Twist and Pull leaning back · Twist and Pull: continuous · Twist and Pull: in triplets · Simple Curl Ups crossing over · Curl Ups crossing over into Arch: bent legs · Curl Ups crossing over into Arch: extended legs

Sideways Arch

also: lateral arch, side bend

Even lateral extension of the whole spine into a long C, not a crease at the waist. Balanced checkpoint: side of neck over the pubic bone.

Used in Sideways Arch · Seated Sideways Arch · Seated Sideways Arch Combination

Narrowing of the pelvis

also: narrow the pelvis, pelvic narrowing

Drawing the pelvis in and together — black thin arrows are the action, gray thick arrows the reaction (shown standing and sitting in the manual). A foundational principle that supports the spine from the base and recurs through the upper-body and abdominal work.

Used in Eagle Combination · Arm Circles in High V · Simple Reaching Behind: double arms · Shoulder Mobilization: sagittal plane

Closing of the ribcage

also: close the ribs, ribcage close

Keeping the front ribs knitting down and in rather than flaring open — especially as the arms travel overhead in the openings.

Used in Overhead Circles: arms · Double Arm Circles: forward · Arm Circles in High V · Eagle Combination

Squeezing breath

also: squeeze breath

The abdominal breath that powers the Curl Up and Jackknife work — a squeezing exhale that hollows and lifts the midsection while the force stays in the diaphragm, not the shoulders.

Used in Simple Curl Ups · Jackknife with feet on the bench · Psoas Abdominals: Single leg changes · Curl Ups: Preparation on the floor · Cradle Combination · Standing on all fours · Curl Ups into Arch: bent legs · Curl Ups into Arch: extended legs · Simple Curl Ups crossing over · Curl Ups crossing over into Arch: bent legs · Curl Ups crossing over into Arch: extended legs · Curl Ups with Leg Combination · Jackknife: Preparation · Jackknife with feet off the bench · Psoas Abdominals: Knee to forehead Combination

Seed center / Yoke

also: yoke, seed center

The deep central core of the body that movements initiate from and connect back to — the arms and legs linking through it to the torso.

Used in Curl Ups with Leg Combination · Eagle Combination · Standing on all fours · Jackknife with feet on the bench

Heart center

also: arms to the heart

The center of the chest; the openings connect the arms to it and it to the seed center and the center of the forehead for a more total being.

Decompression

Creating and maintaining space between the vertebrae throughout the spinal motions — even distribution, never jamming.
